Tajweed Basics

Welcome to our free Tajweed Basics resource! This guide introduces the fundamental rules of Tajweed to help you improve your Quranic recitation. Tajweed is the set of rules governing the pronunciation of each letter with its qualities and observing the rules that apply to those letters when they are in different positions within a word or sentence.

What You'll Learn

  • Introduction to the Arabic alphabet and their pronunciation points
  • Basic Tajweed rules including Ghunnah, Idgham, and Ikhfa
  • Common mistakes in recitation and how to avoid them
  • Practical exercises to improve your recitation

Key Tajweed Rules Covered

  1. Noon Sakinah Rules: Izhar, Idgham, Iqlab, and Ikhfa
  2. Meem Sakinah Rules: Idgham Shafawi, Ikhfa Shafawi, and Izhar Shafawi
  3. Qalqalah: The echoing sound of certain letters
  4. Madd: Elongation rules for vowels

How to Use This Resource

Begin by familiarizing yourself with the Arabic letters and their correct pronunciation. Then, study each Tajweed rule one by one, practicing with the examples provided. Use the audio recordings to hear proper recitation and try to mimic the sounds.

Consistent practice is key to mastering Tajweed. We recommend spending at least 15-20 minutes daily on these exercises for optimal results.
